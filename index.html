Carolina Raffaelli

PhD Candidate in Behavioral Marketing

Rady School of Management, UC San Diego

My research examines judgment and decision-making, with a focus on how people evaluate, acquire, and integrate information when making decisions. One stream of my work studies how consumers think about product usage and how these expectations shape consumption utility, purchasing behavior, and sustainable consumption. For example, I examine whether consumers consider future usage when deciding whether to buy a product, how they weigh duration of ownership versus frequency of use, and whether they accurately predict how long they will use the products they purchase.

A second stream of my research investigates preferences for information acquisition under uncertainty. I study what information people prefer to see first when evaluating decisions involving risk and reward, whether these preferences shape subsequent evaluations, and what mechanisms explain them.
